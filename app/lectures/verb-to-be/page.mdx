# Verb "To Be"

The verb "to be" is one of the most fundamental English verbs. It is an irregular verb that has different meanings depending on the context, but mainly means:

- To exist
- To happen
- To have certain characteristics

Given that this verb is an irregular verb, it has uniquely different forms each one depending on the subject and tense. Let's go through its forms in all simple tenses.

## Present Tense:

In present tense, the form depends on the subject.

- Use "am" with the first person singular (I):
  - "I am a teacher."
  - "I'm good at math."
- Use "is" with the third person singular (he, she, it):
  - "She is a baker."
  - "Maria is a physics teacher."
  - "Luis is in the park."
- Use "are" with the second person (you) and all plural subjects (we, they):
  - "Luis and I are at a birthday party."
  - "You are in school."
  - "We are very smart."

## Past Tense:

In past tense, the form also depends on the subject.

- Use "was" with the first person singular (I) and all third person singular subjects (he, she, it):
  - "I was at a party yesterday."
  - "She was happy with the grade she got."
  - "Pedro was a sportsman; he was actually a policeman."
- Use "were" with the second person (you) and all plural subjects (we, they):
  - "You were a basketball player."
  - "Barbara, Pablo and I were cleaning the house today."
  - "They were very good at biology."

## Future Tense:

In the future tense, the "to be" verb usually comes after the modal auxiliary verb "will" in its base form (as established by modal verbs).

For example:

- She will be a model.
- Camila will be at work tomorrow.
- Juan and Luis will be professional engineers.

## Making negative statements:

To form negative statements from sentences with the verb "to be", you add "not" after the "to be" verb form.

For example:

- I am not a good teacher.
- She isn't a baker.
- Luis and I are not at a birthday party.

## Making questions:

To form questions from sentences with the verb "to be", you invert the verb and the subject.

For example:

- I am a good teacher → Am I a good teacher?
- She isn't a baker → Isn't she a baker?
- Luis and I are at a birthday party → Are Luis and I at a birthday party?
